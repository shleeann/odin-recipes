Sushi Bake Recipe

Description

Sushi bake is the perfect dish to bring with you to parties! It's delicious, easy to make, and the ingredients are readily available at your local supermarket and Asian grocer.

This guaranteed crowd favorite can be prepared within an hour, making it the ideal choice for busybodies and those tight on time. You will only need the following ingredients...

Ingredients:

Sushi Rice

  • 2 cups of uncooked sushi Rice
  • 2 1/4 cups of water
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • Furikake rice seasoning

Salmon & Crab Mix

  • 1 lb salmon
  • 1 lb imitation crab meat
  • 4 oz cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup Kewpie mayo
  • Sriracha to taste
  • Green onions

Topping

  • Kewpie mayo
  • Sriracha
  • Furikake rice seasoning

Instructions

Sushi Rice

  1. Rinse rice to rid of the starch, then add to rice cooker with water and set to cook.
  2. Once rice is cooked, place rice in mixing bowl and add a teaspoon of sesame oil to taste, gently fold.
  3. Sprinkle in some furikake rice seasoning, and gently fold the mixture again.
  4. Deposit rice into an oven-safe casserole dish and spread evenly. Sprinkle a layer of furikake atop the rice and set aside.

Salmon & Crab Mix

  1. Preheat oven to 400F.
  2. Place salmon on greased baking pan and place in oven once oven is done preheating (temperature should be at 400F). Let bake for 15 minutes.
  3. While salmon is baking, start chopping up imitation crab meat into small quarter inch chunks (or run through food processor, if available).
  4. Once salmon is done baking, place on a plate and start shredding into smaller pieces, then transfer into mixing bowl with the chopped up imitation crab meat.
  5. In the mixing bowl, add the cream cheese, half a cup of Kewpie mayo, and your desired amount of sriracha to taste. Fold and mix well with the salmon and imitation crab meat.
  6. Chop up green onions and add to mixing bowl, fold to mix.

Sushi Bake

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Take salmon and crab mixture and spread a thick, even layer atop the sushi rice prepared earlier.
  3. Sprinkle furikake rice seasoning atop the salmon and crab layer, and, if you so desire, drizzle some Kewpie mayo and sriracha on top for added taste.
  4. Bake for 35 minutes.

Enjoy a hot and delicious sushi bake casserole with piece of nori seaweed for the full experience!
